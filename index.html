About The Cancer Lady, RN

A nurse who has been on both sides of the fight.

My Story

On March 1, 2023, at approximately 9:10 AM, I sat in my gynecologist’s office wondering why he was running late. I was his first appointment of the day.

He wasn’t running late. He was on the phone with the pathologist.

When he walked in, everything changed. In a single sentence, I went from a nurse, a mother, an educator — to a cancer patient. My son was 11 years old.

"The first call I made wasn’t to a specialist. It was to my pastor. I didn’t need a treatment plan yet. I needed a Word from God. He told me this was not going to be the end for me."

And I believed him — because I knew that God had called me to nursing. He had called me to education. He had called me to serve. And He wasn’t done with me yet.

Today my son is 14. He has watched his mother fight. He has watched his mother pray. And he has watched his mother get back up.

That combination of clinical expertise and lived experience means I don’t just understand the healthcare system — I understand what it feels like to sit on the other side of it.

Why I Started This

Through my own journey, I learned something that changed everything: the healthcare system isn’t built to hold your hand through the hardest season of your life. Clinicians are doing extraordinary work — but they can’t do everything within a 15-minute visit.

I started The Cancer Lady, RN because I remember what it felt like to need someone who understood both the system and the fear — and not have her.

Now I want to be that person for others. I walk alongside cancer patients and their families through every step of the journey — sitting with my clients through their appointments and treatments, asking the tough questions, coordinating care, and making sure nothing falls through the cracks.

Shoulder to shoulder. Not from a distance.

Our Services

Comprehensive patient advocacy and care management for every stage of the journey.

01

Patient Advocacy

Your voice matters in every treatment decision. We make sure it’s heard.

We sit with our clients through their appointments and treatments — asking the questions they didn’t know to ask, taking notes so they don’t have to, and translating complex medical information into language that makes sense.

  • Appointment accompaniment (in-person or virtual)
  • Treatment plan review and explanation
  • Medical jargon translation
  • Second opinion support and coordination
  • Ensuring patient preferences are communicated
02

Care Coordination

When you have four specialists and none of them are talking to each other — that’s where we come in.

We manage the communication between your providers, organize your schedule, navigate insurance hurdles, and make sure every piece of your care plan works together — so you can focus on healing.

  • Multi-provider communication management
  • Appointment scheduling and organization
  • Insurance navigation and prior authorizations
  • Medical records coordination
  • Treatment timeline management
03

Care Navigation

From the moment of diagnosis through survivorship, we are with you every step of the way.

Whether you need help understanding your options, exploring clinical trials, seeking a second opinion, or planning for what comes next — you don’t have to figure it out alone.

  • Diagnosis-to-survivorship guidance
  • Clinical trial research and eligibility review
  • Healthcare system navigation
  • End-of-life planning support

Understanding Your Support Team
Patient Advocate. Care Manager. Care Navigator.
Three roles. One mission: making sure you never face the system alone.
♥

Patient Advocate

Your voice in the room. A patient advocate sits with you during appointments and treatments, asks the questions you didn't know to ask, ensures your wishes are heard, and translates medical jargon into language that makes sense.

Think of it as: someone who speaks up for you when the system gets loud.

⚙

Care Manager

Your behind-the-scenes coordinator. A care manager connects the dots between your doctors, organizes your schedule, handles insurance hurdles, manages your medical records, and makes sure nothing falls through the cracks.

Think of it as: someone who keeps all the moving pieces working together.

⊕

Care Navigator

Your guide through the journey. A care navigator helps you understand your options from diagnosis through survivorship — researching clinical trials, exploring second opinions, and planning for what comes next at every stage.

Think of it as: someone who lights the path ahead so you're never walking blind.

At The Cancer Lady, RN, we provide all three — because your journey deserves more than one kind of support.
